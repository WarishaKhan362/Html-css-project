Indian Dance Culture

Dance forms in India are as diverse as it's languages. Different dance forms have come forth from various states. Dance in India is more than just body movements. It is more than art and a lot about expression with notes. It is also a way to discipline the self with compassion and devote one's entire self to God.

North India Dance


Dumhal - Jammu and Kashmir

Kathak. A dance of northern India, Kathak is often a dance of love. It is performed by both men and women. The movements include intricate footwork accented by bells worn around the ankles and stylized gestures adapted from normal body language.

Nati - Himachal Pradesh.

The word Nati is used for the traditional folk songs sung in the Western and Central Hills of the Indian subcontinent. It is primarily native to the states of Himachal Pradesh and Uttarakhand.

South India Dance


kuchipudi - Andhra Pradeshr

kuchipudi, one of six classical dance styles of India. Kuchipudi is indigenous to the state of Andhra Pradesh and differs from the other five classical styles by the inclusion of singing.

Yakshagana - Karnataka

What are the famous dances in Karnataka? Dollu Kunitha and Yakshagana are two of Karnataka's most well-known folk dances. Yakshagana is a Karnataka traditional theatre dance, whereas Dollu Kunitha is a Karnataka ritualistic dance.

Kathakali - Kerala

Kathakali is a major form of classical Indian dance. It is a "story play" genre of art, but one distinguished by the elaborately colourful make-up and costumes of the traditional male actor-dancers.
